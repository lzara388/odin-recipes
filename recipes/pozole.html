Pozole

Description:

Pozole (pronounced pow.zowl.e) is a traditional Mexican stew made from hominy and a choice of either chicken or pork. Because it is a festive dish, it is typically served on special occasions, such as birthdays, or holidays like Christmas Eve or New Year's Eve.

There three version of pozole:

  • white (blanco)
  • green (verde)
  • red (rojo) - What we'll be making

Red pozole is prepared with a spicy red sause that includes a variety of red chilies, onion, and garlic. Green pozole, on the other hand, is prepared with a variety of green chilies, cilantro, garlic, and onion. In contrast, white pozole is just the chicken/pork broth without the addition of any sauce. Pozole is served with a variety of toppings including chopped onions, shredded cabbage, sliced radish, lime, and more chilies/sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of your choice of protien (replace with 2 cans of pinto beans for vegetarian version)
  • 45 oz of white hominy, rinsed and drained
  • Kosher salt
  • 3/4 cup of dried chiles de arbol
  • 4 or 5 dried ancho chiles
  • 6 cloved of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 1 large yellow onion
  • 3 bay leafs
  • 1 tablespoon (or 1 cube) of bouilon
  • 3 tablespoons of oregano

    • For Topping (optional):

  • sliced radish, shredded cabbage, diced onioin, lime, cilantro

Steps

  1. Break stems of chilies and shake out as many seeds as possible. Boil chilies in a bot of water and soak for 10 minutes. Transfer the chilies and water into a blender and blend until liquid. Add 2 cloves of garlic and a pinch of salt and blend until smooth. Pour liquid into a fine-mesh sieve over a bowl. Push sauce through with a spatula and discarf solids.
  2. In another pot heat oil on medium heat and add choice of protien. Add onions and a pinch of salt, and stir occiasionally for about 5 minutes (or until onions become soft). Add the rest of garlic and cook until protien begins to brown on all sides.
  3. Add 2 cups of water, bouilon, bay leafs, oregano, and a pinch of salt, as well as your blended sauce. Bring this combination to a low boil, then reduce to a simmer and cover for about 2.5 hours, stirring occasionally until protien becomes tender.
  4. Stir in hominy and continue to simmer for another hour (or until protien starts falling apart). Remove bay leafs. If stock is too thick, add more water. Season with salt and serve with assorted toppings!
